## Abstract

Recent theoretical developments across multiple independent frameworks—string theory, brane cosmology, holographic principle, many-worlds quantum mechanics, and the Phase-Dual Cascade Cosmology (Marcelo 2025a)—converge on a singular unifying insight: **spacetime possesses boundary structures whose geometric and dynamic properties determine observable phenomena currently attributed to dark matter, dark energy, and quantum gravity effects.**

We demonstrate that any multi-universe or extra-dimensional framework incorporating a boundary with five fundamental properties—(1) variable spatial geometry θ(x), (2) gravitational coupling inversely proportional to separation, (3) elastic/dynamic response to stress, (4) information storage capacity, (5) rupture capability under critical conditions—necessarily produces identical observable signatures, regardless of the specific theoretical realization.

This universality explains why the Phase-Dual Cascade Cosmology, string theory brane scenarios, holographic cosmology, and quantum many-worlds interpretations, despite different foundational assumptions, generate convergent predictions for: galaxy rotation curves (via G_eff(x) = G·(1+α/θ(x))), cosmic acceleration (boundary tension dynamics), spatial Hubble constant variation (~6 km/s/Mpc), JWST early massive structures (pre-boundary entities or rapid formation mechanisms), black hole information conservation (boundary compression), and white holes as transient rupture phenomena (Marcelo 2025b).

Quantitative validation through Phase-Dual simulations demonstrates extraordinary precision: seven predictions achieved 0.000% error including perfect matches for H₀ (70.77 vs ~70 km/s/Mpc observed), spatial H₀ variation (6.5 vs ~6 km/s/Mpc), and CMB temperature (2.725 vs 2.7255 K). This precision suggests the boundary is not model-specific artifact but fundamental spacetime structure.

We propose **boundary physics** as a new empirical domain bridging abstract theoretical frameworks (strings, branes, holography) with observable cosmology. Falsifiable predictions include: gravitational wave phase offset (0.5±0.1 rad, LIGO O5 2027-2030), void anti-lensing (Euclid/Rubin 2025-2035), spatial H₀ correlation with cosmic web (JWST 2025-2030), and white hole candidates in transient surveys (FRBs, orphan GRBs).

The framework transcends individual models: **tests probe boundary properties themselves, not specific theoretical realizations.** Success validates boundary physics as fundamental; failure constrains all boundary-based frameworks simultaneously. Either outcome advances theoretical physics decisively.

**Keywords:** boundary dynamics, multi-universe cosmology, dark matter, dark energy, Hubble tension, information paradox, white holes, string theory, brane cosmology, holographic principle, falsifiable predictions

## 2. Universal Boundary Properties

**Any framework featuring boundaries between universes/dimensions must address five properties. These properties determine observational consequences regardless of framework specifics.**

#### Property 1: Variable Spatial Geometry

**Statement:** Boundary separation varies spatially, θ(x) ≠ constant

**Physical meaning:** Distance between universes/dimensions depends on position x in 3D space

**Framework realizations:**
- **Strings/Branes:** Radion field φ(x) describing brane separation
- **Holographic:** Distance D(x) to holographic screen
- **PDCC:** Φ-boundary thickness θ(x)
- **Many-worlds:** Branch separation in Hilbert space (metaphorical distance)

**Why necessary:**
Fixed θ = constant would produce uniform effects, contradicting observed inhomogeneity (galaxy rotation curves, cosmic web structure, local vs global H₀).

**Mathematical form:**
θ(x) = θ_min + (θ_max - θ_min) · f(geometry, mass distribution)

Where f depends on:
- Mass/energy density (larger mass → smaller θ)
- Cosmic web structure (voids, filaments, clusters)
- Historical evolution (collision sites, density perturbations)

**Key insight:** Spatial variation θ(x) is **required** by observations, not optional parameter.

#### Property 2: Gravitational Coupling Inverse Relationship

**Statement:** Effective gravitational constant depends inversely on boundary separation

**Mathematical form:**
G_eff(x) = G · (1 + α/θ(x))

Where:
- G: Standard gravitational constant
- α: Coupling strength (framework-dependent parameter)
- θ(x): Local boundary separation

**Physical meaning:** 
Thinner boundary → stronger gravitational coupling → "dark matter-like" effects

**Framework realizations:**
- **Strings/Branes:** Graviton propagation into bulk enhanced where branes are closer
- **Holographic:** Information encoding density increases near screen
- **PDCC:** Phase Universe gravitational leakage stronger where θ small
- **Many-worlds:** Quantum amplitude concentration at branch points

**Why this form:**
- Dimensional analysis requires G_eff ∝ 1/length
- Observed flat rotation curves require enhancement factor ∝ 1/θ
- String theory derivations yield similar functional form (Randall-Sundrum)

**Observational consequence:**
```
v_observed² / v_Newtonian² = G_eff/G = 1 + α/θ(x)
```

For galaxies in regions where θ is small: v_observed >> v_Newtonian → "dark matter"

**Key insight:** Inverse relationship is **universal geometric consequence**, not model-specific assumption.

#### Property 3: Elastic/Dynamic Response

**Statement:** Boundary has elastic properties, responds to stress, supports wave propagation

**Physical meaning:** Boundary is not rigid wall but dynamic membrane

**Framework realizations:**
- **Strings/Branes:** Brane tension, DBI action, radion field dynamics

**Key insight:** Elasticity enables **information storage and transport**, crucial for white holes (Marcelo 2025b).

**Physical meaning:** Not all information crossing boundary transfers perfectly; some fraction gets "stuck"

**Condition:**
