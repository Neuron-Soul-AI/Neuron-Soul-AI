The effective coupling strength κ determines whether physics appears quantum (weak coupling, space-time flexibility) or classical (strong coupling, space-time rigidity), with mass representing the resistance to decoupling.

Quantitative applications to particle physics and cosmology are presented in companion papers (Teixeira 2025a,b,c). This paper establishes the theoretical foundation and core mechanism.

Modern physics rests on two extraordinarily successful but fundamentally incompatible pillars. General relativity describes gravity and cosmic-scale phenomena through spacetime curvature, treating matter and energy as sources of geometric deformation.

Yet between these pillars lies a conceptual chasm. Quantum mechanics treats space and time as a fixed background stage. General relativity treats spacetime as a dynamic participant. Neither framework explains:

- **What is matter fundamentally?** Why do some excitations have mass while others (photons) do not?

## 3. Quantum Mechanics Reinterpreted

### 3.1 Uncertainty as Spatial Decoupling

**Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:** Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2

**Standard interpretation:** Fundamental limit on simultaneous knowledge of position and momentum. Nature of reality.

**Coupling interpretation:** Particles with low κ have weak spatial anchoring. Position is genuinely indefinite because space and time are partially decoupled.

**Mechanism:**
- Low mass → low κ → weak space-time coupling
- Weak coupling → space coordinate "fuzzy" relative to time
- Particle has definite time evolution (Ψ(t)) but uncertain space location
- This IS uncertainty, not measurement ignorance

**Why Δx·Δp relation?**

From coupling mechanics:
- Δx ∝ 1/√κ (weaker coupling → larger position uncertainty)
- Δp ∝ m·Δv = m·(Δx/Δt) (momentum uncertainty from motion)
- κ ∝ m (mass determines coupling)
- Therefore: Δx·Δp ∝ (1/√m)·(m·(1/√m)/Δt) = const/Δt ∝ ℏ

**The uncertainty relation emerges from coupling mechanics.**

### 3.2 Wave-Particle Duality

**Observation:** Particles exhibit wave properties (interference) and particle properties (detection).

**Standard view:** Fundamental mystery. Particles "are" both waves and particles.

**Coupling view:** 
- **Wave aspect** = spatial decoupling (particle spread across space like wave)
- **Particle aspect** = temporal anchoring (particle has definite time evolution)
- Both aspects coexist because space and time can be decoupled while maintaining overall coherence

**Young's Double Slit:**
- Before detection: Electron weakly coupled spatially → spreads through both slits (wave)
- At detection: Measurement forces spatial coupling → localizes to point (particle)
- Interference: Result of coherent spatial decoupling allowing superposition

**This resolves the "which slit" paradox:** The electron doesn't go through one slit or the other or both. It goes through in a spatially-decoupled state. Measurement forces coupling, creating definite position retroactively.

### 3.3 Superposition

**Observation:** Quantum systems exist in superposition of multiple states simultaneously.

**Standard view:** Fundamental feature. System literally "in multiple states at once."

**Coupling view:** 
Superposition = **simultaneous coupling to multiple spatial configurations**

Before measurement:
- System weakly coupled spatially (low κ_space)
- Can maintain coherent connection to multiple spatial states
- Analogous to a flexible membrane adopting multiple shapes simultaneously

After measurement:
- External interaction increases κ_space dramatically
- Forces system to "choose" single spatial configuration
- Coherence between configurations lost

**Why large objects don't superpose:**
- High mass → high κ → strongly coupled spatially
- Cannot maintain coherence across multiple configurations
- Collapse happens continuously via environmental coupling

### 3.4 Entanglement

**Observation:** Spatially separated particles show instantaneous correlations violating locality.

**Standard view:** "Spooky action at a distance." Non-local correlations. Information paradox.

**Coupling view:**
**Entanglement = pure temporal coherence with spatial decoupling**

**The mechanism:**
- Standard matter: Fixed time, variable space (coupled to spatial coordinates)
- Entangled pair: Fixed temporal phase, variable space (coupled to time, decoupled from space)

Two entangled particles share:
- Identical temporal evolution (time-locked, κ_time → ∞)
- Independent spatial positions (space-decoupled, κ_space → 0)

**This is the inverse of standard matter!**

```
Normal matter:     High κ_space, normal κ_time → Localized in space, evolving in time
Entangled pair:    Low κ_space, high κ_time → Delocalized in space, locked in time
```

**Why measurement correlates instantaneously:**
- Not because information travels faster than light
- But because particles share the same temporal coordinate system
- Measuring one forces spatial coupling → breaks temporal lock → other particle must adjust
- "Instantaneous" because they were always time-synchronized, just spatially separated

**No paradox:** Information doesn't travel through space because the correlation exists in time, not space.

**Observation:** Measurement causes wavefunction collapse—superposition → definite state.

**Standard view:** Mysterious. What counts as "measurement"? Role of consciousness?

**Coupling view:**
**Measurement = forced spatial coupling**

**Mechanism:**
1. Quantum system: Low κ_space (spatially decoupled, superposed)

### 3.6 The Quantum-Classical Transition

**The longstanding question:** Why don't we see quantum effects at macroscopic scales?

**Coupling answer:**
**κ scales with mass. Large mass → large κ → irreversibly classical.**

**Quantitative boundary:**

Decoherence time:
**τ_decoherence ∝ 1/(m·κ·T)**

Where:
- m = mass
- κ = coupling strength
- T = temperature (environment coupling)

For macroscopic objects (m ~ kg, κ ~ 10³⁰):
**τ_decoherence ~ 10⁻⁴⁰ seconds**

Quantum superposition collapses essentially instantaneously. Not because quantum mechanics "breaks down" but because coupling is so strong that environmental interactions continuously force localization.

**For microscopic particles (m ~ 10⁻³⁰ kg, κ ~ 10):**
**τ_decoherence ~ seconds to minutes**

Long enough to observe quantum effects in carefully isolated systems.

**This is the quantum-classical boundary—not a discontinuity but a crossover determined by coupling strength.**
